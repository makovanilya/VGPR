# Contributing to VGPR

## How to Submit a New Rating

1. **Open an Issue** using the "New Rating Submission" template.
2. Fill in all fields. Partial submissions will be marked NR until complete.
3. Every criterion must be backed by a **cited source** (news article, official announcement, patch notes, etc.).
4. A rating is considered final after **7 days with no dispute**.

## How to Dispute a Rating

1. Open a **Discussion** (not an Issue) with the title: `[DISPUTE] Game Title | Reason`
2. State specifically which criterion you believe is incorrectly applied.
3. Provide counter-evidence with sources.
4. Disputes are resolved by consensus, not by a single moderator.

## What Counts as a Valid Source

- ✅ Official developer/publisher announcements.
- ✅ Archived patch notes.
- ✅ Reputable gaming journalism (IGN, Eurogamer, PC Gamer etc.) or credible news articles. 
- ✅ Web Archive captures of official pages.
- ✅ Legal filings or regulatory documents.

- ❌ Forum posts or Reddit threads (unless citing an official source).
- ❌ YouTube videos (unless an official developer statement is recorded).
- ❌ Personal experience without corroboration.

## Style Rules for Rating Cards

- Dates in ISO format: YYYY-MM-DD
- Game titles exactly as they appear on storefronts.
- Sources listed in chronological order.
- No editorializing in criteria checklists, use facts only (advocacy belongs in the skg-alignment.md doc, not rating cards).

## Spec Changes

If you believe a modifier or tier definition should change:
1. Open a Discussion titled `[SPEC] Proposed Change | Description`.
2. Changes to the spec increment the version number (v1.0 → v1.1).
3. Existing ratings are not retroactively re-rated, they retain their spec version tag.
