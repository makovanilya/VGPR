# Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who decides the ratings?**  
A: It is decided by the community and respectable sources they provide.

**Q: What if a game is partially preserved by fans but not officially?**  
A: Rate what the *publisher* did, not what the community achieved. A game may be available thanks to its community but the ratings reflect actions of the publishers. 
You can note what community achieved and what friction they dealt with.

**Q: Can a game's rating change over time?**  
A: Yes. If a publisher later releases a sunset patch or source code, the rating is updated. The change is logged in the rating card timeline.

**Q: Why CC0 and not a more restrictive license?**  
A: Maximum adoption is the goal. Journalists, regulators and advocacy groups should be able to use this data without any legal friction.

**Q: Is this anti-publisher?**  
A: Not at all. Publishers that handle EoL responsibly are recognized for it. This system rewards good behavior as much as it documents bad behavior.
